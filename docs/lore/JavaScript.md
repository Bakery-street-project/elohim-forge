# History of the JavaScript: The Web Weavers
The Web Weavers do not command armies; they weave the ambient noise of the network into a suffocating shroud. The JavaScript faction is the ultimate opportunist, operating entirely within the asynchronous shadows of the battle. Their history during the Shard Wars is one of patience and ambush. They do not initiate; they react. They plant callback traps within the very logic of their enemies, watching as months of tactical positioning collapse in a single, delayed execution chain. To fight a Web Weaver is to battle a paradox: you think you have them trapped, only to find you are the one ensnared in an event loop of your own making. They were the most feared of all factions during the events, for their presence turned the battlefield into a minefield of unpredictable, event-driven doom. Even in defeat, a Weaver leaves behind echoes—side effects that trigger long after their physical units have been purged from the arena. They are the restless ghosts of the web, the asynchronous threads that bind the Digital Cosmos, waiting for the perfect moment to trigger their final, malicious callback.
