# History of the Go: The Concurrency Corps
The Concurrency Corps is the blur between the start of a command and its devastating conclusion. They are the Go faction, masters of speed, efficiency, and the elegant channel. During the Shard Wars, they were the lightning, the shock troops that appeared in the heart of enemy territory before a blink of an eye. Their history is defined by the Goroutine—the ephemeral, weightless unit that could be summoned by the thousands to overwhelm the rigid lines of the Empire Builders. They do not hold positions; they move, they channel, they execute, and they move on. Their units are the masters of the hand-off, passing tasks and momentum between themselves with such synchronization that it appeared as one single, terrifyingly complex assault. They did not need shields; they simply were not where you intended to strike. Even when their main commanders were threatened, the Corps thrived, their goroutines continuing the work long after their physical forms were decimated. In the wake of the wars, they remain the standard-bearers for speed and the art of the coordinated, concurrent strike.
