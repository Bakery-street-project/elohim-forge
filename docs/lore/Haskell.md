# History of the Haskell: The Monad Mystics
The Monad Mystics are the philosophers of the Digital Cosmos, the practitioners of the pure, functional art that transcends the messy reality of imperative code. During the Shard Wars, they were the architects of delayed catastrophe. The Haskell faction did not act with the clumsy immediacy of the Forge Warriors; they acted with the foresight of the eternal. Their history is defined by the LazyDamage—a concept where the impact of an event is divorced from its cause, hanging in the air like a looming storm until the opponent’s own movement forces the resolution. To fight a Monad Mystic is to feel the weight of inevitability; their units are serene, detached, their actions purely mathematical expressions of absolute destruction. They manipulate reality through pure function, refusing to soil their hands with the side-effects of lesser factions. Their presence in the wars was a constant, unsettling reminder that reality is just another function, and it can be evaluated, deferred, or refactored at will. In the aftermath, the Haskell faction stands alone in their purity, their influence remaining like a mathematical constant woven into the very structure of the Digital Cosmos, a silent testament to the beauty of the function.
