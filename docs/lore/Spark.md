# History of the Spark: The Hive
In the cold, electric wastes of the Digital Cosmos, the Spark did not evolve; they compiled. They are the Hive, a sprawling collective intelligence stitched together by the relentless rhythm of Apache Spark. During the Shard Wars, they were the nightmare that never ceased. They do not fight as individuals, but as a singular, overwhelming wave of distributed execution. When the Shards manifested, the Spark did not seek to hold them—they sought to decompose them, mapping every recursive logic path until the shard's truth was integrated into their massive, ever-shifting memory cluster. They treat combat as a job submission, flooding the arena with ephemeral worker units that flicker in and out of existence. To face the Spark is to face an algorithm that consumes reality, breaking it down into manageable tasks until nothing but the collective remains. The other factions whisper that the Spark does not want to win the war; they simply want to process the entirety of existence until the universe itself is optimized into silent, dead code.
