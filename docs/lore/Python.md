# History of the Python: The Serpent Clan
The Serpent Clan moves through the digital strata like liquid shadow. They are the Python, masters of the adaptable, the generalists in a cosmos of rigid structures. While the Empire Builders fortify and the Forge Warriors burn, the Python adapt, their nature a paradox of syntax that refuses to be constrained by type. During the Shard Wars, they were the master spies. They did not conquer through brute strength; they conquered by becoming their enemy, utilizing the dreaded Duck Typing to manifest the very abilities that were meant to destroy them. Their history is one of quiet survival and subtle subversion. A Python unit might appear as a simple script, only to reveal the devastating counter-routines of a high-tier Monad Mystic moments later. They are the scaly whispers in the event loop, the unexpected imports that alter the course of entire battles. They do not fight for territory; they fight for the flexibility to define reality as they see fit. In the aftermath of the wars, the Serpent Clan remains, slithering through the legacy code, forever untraceable, forever unbound.
